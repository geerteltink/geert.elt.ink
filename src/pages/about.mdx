<h1>I am here to help you create structure in your IT</h1>

I am a developer, designer, architect, and leader. I not only build your
website or application, I can make it look fantastic, publish it and even build
the team that is needed to get it done.

## Study

I left my hometown in the Netherlands, and stopped in Enschede to give it a try
at a bachelors degree in applied sciences (Business Information Management).

## Freelance

After my study, I found my way to the South of Spain where I served entrepreneurs
who are also following their unconventional dreams for the next 20 years.

## Career at the big shots

Unfortenatly the entrepreneurs I worked with and made it a sport to make me
chase for money, and I decided to move on. With all the experience I had, I
found a new challenge in Munich at [ottonova](https://www.ottonova.de/) were I
started as a senior software enigneer. A real challenging work as they were the
first to fully digitalize the private health insurance. At the first
oppertunity, I got promoted to team lead software engineering. And a year later
I built and lead a new cross functional team. At the same time I was the tech
lead to bring BKV (Betriebliche Krankenversicherung) to ottonova.

After 3.5 years I had the oppertunity to became a Head of Development at XPAY.
A promising startup which sells branded loyalty debit cards. Amongst others,
web a special shiny metal card for Sakia Dietz, a MasterChef Italy card, and a
FoodCard together with telepizza. I was in charge the structure the IT teams
and bring their infrastructure to the next level event driven micro services
for real time processing of payments.

The job at XPAY was very challenging, as was their financial status.
Unfortenatly, they had the same destiny as a lot of other startups with great
ideas and I found myself back at ottonova. First as a senior software engineer
and after two months once again as a software engineering manager.

## Sharing

I've learned a thing or two over the years when working as a developer. I love
sharing what I've learned and use this site for that.

> The difference between knowledge and wisdom is experience.

I choose a skill I want to be good at, do the research and will get my hands
dirty while experimenting to uncover its principles. To be able to write or
teach I need real experience.

## So, how can I help you?

From architecture, to development and building teams. I am your next Tech Lead, Head of
Development or CTO.
